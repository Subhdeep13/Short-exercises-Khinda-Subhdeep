Andrew Ross Sorkin, author of Too Big to Fail

November

New York Times columnist and author, Andrew Ross Sorkin, has been described as “the most famous financial journalist of his generation.” A leading voice on Wall Street and corporate America, his New York Times bestseller, Too Big to Fail, was the first, behind-the-scenes, moment-by-moment account of how that financial crisis developed into a global tsunami.

The book inspired the HBO film of the same name, and Sorkin’s work earned Too Big to Fail one of the best book of the year titles. The book was published by Viking October 20, 2009. The book was adapted as a movie by HBO Films and premiered on HBO on May 23, 2011. The film was directed by Curtis Hanson, and the screenplay was written by Peter Gould.

Praise for Too Big to Fail

“Vigorously reported, superbly organized ... for those of us who didn’t pursue MBAs and have the stomachs to match.”
— Julia Keller, Chicago Tribune

“Sorkin’s prodigious reporting and lively writing get the reader in the room for some of the biggest-dollar conference calls in history ... It’s an entertaining, essential book.”
— Paul M. Barrett, The New York Times Book Review
